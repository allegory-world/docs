Writing is Scout's content generation mode. It drafts grant proposals and funding application responses that are grounded in your organisation's actual profile, past documents, and the specific requirements of the funder.

Use Writing when you're ready to produce application content—whether starting from scratch, expanding a section, or refining a draft already in progress.

## How Writing works

When you ask Scout to write, it starts immediately. It does not present an outline and wait—it produces the content and you can redirect or refine from there.

Behind the scenes, Scout silently gathers the context it needs in the same step as writing:

- **Your organisation's profile** — name, focus areas, geography, industry, and any other stored details
- **The funder's profile and priorities** — from Allegory's database and live web if needed
- **Your documents** — anything marked as active in AI Context, such as past proposals, impact reports, or mission statements
- **The application form** — if the opportunity has an imported form, Scout uses its questions and structure to frame the draft

Every claim Scout makes is grounded in a real source. It cites inline and does not fabricate statistics, capabilities, or history.

## What you can ask Scout to write

- Full proposal drafts for a specific grant or call
- Individual sections (e.g., "Draft the organisation overview section")
- Responses to specific application form questions
- Revised or expanded versions of existing content ("Make this section stronger" / "Shorten this to 200 words")
- Content matched to a specific funder's stated priorities

## Iterating on drafts

Writing is designed for back-and-forth. After Scout produces a draft:

- Ask it to revise specific passages ("Make this paragraph more persuasive")
- Ask it to adjust tone or length ("Shorten to fit a 500-word limit")
- Ask it to add a missing element ("Add a section on our previous funding experience")
- Copy sections directly into the Proposal tab of your Funding Tracker to continue editing collaboratively with your team

<Tip>
  For the best output, make sure your key organisational documents—impact reports, previous proposals, mission statements—are uploaded and set as active in AI Context before you start a writing session.
</Tip>
