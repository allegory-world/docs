title: "Ask"
description: "Get fast, sourced answers to questions about grants, funders, and your pipeline without leaving Allegory."
---

Ask is Scout's default mode. It is designed for quick, accurate answers—Scout responds concisely and cites every claim inline so you can follow up or verify directly.

Use Ask when you need to understand something quickly: a funder's priorities, whether your organisation is eligible for a specific grant, what a deadline means, or a summary of your current pipeline.

## What Scout can answer

Scout answers questions by combining several sources simultaneously:

- **Your organisation's profile** — focus areas, geography, industry, and team data
- **Your saved funders and tracked opportunities** — details already in your Funding Tracker
- **Your documents** — anything uploaded and marked as AI Context in the Documents section
- **Allegory's funder database** — detailed profiles on tens of thousands of grant-making organisations
- **Live web search** — current news, funder announcements, and grant information not yet in the database

## Example questions

- *"What are this funder's stated priorities for 2026?"*
- *"Summarise our current pipeline"*
- *"Is our organisation eligible for Horizon Europe given our location?"*
- *"What is the difference between an SBIR Phase I and Phase II award?"*
- *"Which of our saved funders focus on clean energy in North America?"*

## Keeping answers brief

Ask is calibrated for brevity. Responses are concise by default—Scout answers in a few sentences and gets to the point. If you need more detail, ask a follow-up question or explicitly request an expanded answer.

## When to switch modes

Ask will suggest switching modes when your request calls for it:

- **Search** — when you want Scout to actively discover new funding opportunities across multiple databases
- **Writing** — when you want Scout to draft or expand proposal content
