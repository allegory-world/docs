title: "Search"
description: "Run deep, multi-source research to discover new funding opportunities across global grant databases, government portals, and the live web."
---

Search is Scout's deep research mode. Rather than answering a single question, Search actively hunts for funding opportunities across multiple databases and sources in parallel, verifies each result, and presents a consolidated, sourced list.

Use Search when you want Scout to go out and find opportunities you haven't discovered yet—rather than answer questions about what you already have.

## How Search works

When you describe what you're looking for, Scout searches all relevant sources at the same time. Each programme found is verified before being surfaced: Scout crawls the source URL to confirm the opportunity is still open and the details are accurate. Results that are closed, expired, or unreachable are silently dropped.

### Sources Scout searches

| Source | What it covers |
|--------|---------------|
| **Allegory funder database** | Tens of thousands of grant-making organisations and their known programmes |
| **Your organisation's saved data** | Funders and opportunities already in your tracker |
| **Your documents** | Context from files you have marked as active in AI Context |
| **Live web** | Current funder announcements, open calls, and grant news |
| **Grants.gov** | US federal grants and cooperative agreements |
| **SAM.gov** | US government procurement and federal assistance |
| **SBIR/STTR** | Small Business Innovation Research and Technology Transfer solicitations |
| **EU Funding & Tenders (SEDIA)** | Horizon Europe, European Defence Fund, Digital Europe, and other EU framework programmes |
| **PARP** | Polish Agency for Enterprise Development grant programmes |
| *More sources coming soon* | |

## Saving opportunities

Once Scout surfaces results, you can ask it to save any programme directly to your Funding Tracker. Scout will present an **Approve / Reject** prompt before writing anything—confirm to add the opportunity with its details pre-populated, or reject to skip it.

## Tips for better results

- **Be specific.** The more context you give—geography, eligibility type, funding amount range, sector—the more targeted the results.
- **Reference what you already have.** You can ask Scout to find opportunities similar to one already in your tracker, or to match the profile of a specific document. For example: *"Find me grants similar to the Horizon Europe opportunity in my tracker"* or *"Find funding that fits the organisation described in our impact report."*
- **Iterate.** After an initial search, ask Scout to narrow down, filter by deadline, or focus on a specific source.

## When to switch modes

After a Search, Scout may suggest switching to Writing if you want to start drafting an application for one of the discovered programmes.
