Scout is a persistent AI agent that lives in the **Scout** section of your left-hand sidebar. Unlike a standard chatbot, Scout maintains state across conversations—it remembers what it has researched, which opportunities it has found, and what it is currently drafting.

## Modes

Scout has three modes, each optimised for a different task. Switch between them using the mode picker in the prompt bar.

| Mode | Best for |
|------|----------|
| [**Ask**](/scout/ask) | Quick questions about grants, funders, or your pipeline |
| [**Search**](/scout/search) | Deep, multi-source research to discover new opportunities |
| [**Writing**](/scout/writing) | Drafting and refining grant proposals and applications |

Scout will also suggest switching modes when your request is better handled by a different one—for example, it may recommend switching to Search when you ask it to find opportunities, or to Writing when you ask it to draft a section.

## Sessions

Every conversation is saved as a session. Your session history appears in the collapsible panel on the right side of the Scout page. Each session is automatically titled based on the first exchange.

You can start a new session at any time or return to a previous one to continue where you left off. Scout resumes with full memory of what was discussed in that session.

## Approvals

Some actions—such as saving a discovered opportunity to your Funding Tracker or updating an existing one—require your explicit approval before Scout executes them. When this happens, Scout presents an **Approve / Reject** prompt in the conversation. Approving confirms the action; rejecting cancels it with no changes made.

## Context from your documents

Scout automatically draws on documents you have marked as active in the AI Context column of your [Documents](/documents/managing-documents) page. The more relevant material you add there, the more grounded and accurate Scout's research and drafting will be.
